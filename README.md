# Movie-Dialogue-Sentiment-Analysis
## By Hoang Viet Phan

“Two Voices, Two Perspectives”: Men and Women in Movie Dialogue

There has been extensive research into whether men and women speak differently across different contexts. My project sought to answer this question in the context of movie dialogue. I extracted data from Cornell Movie-Dialogs Corpus and analyzed how dialogue sentiments differ across different gender groupings over multiple decades.
I found that: 
1. women have significantly higher dialogue sentiments;
2. sentiments of dialogue between same-gender speakers are significantly lower than between opposite-gender speakers.

I conclude that these speech sentiment differences have been consistent over time, which may stem from social implications related to gender and how we speak to in-group versus out-group people.
